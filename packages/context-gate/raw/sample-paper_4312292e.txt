A retrospective cohort study was conducted at Ankara University Faculty of Medicine
between January 2022 and December 2024. A total of 245 patients diagnosed with
atrial fibrillation were enrolled.

Patient demographics:
- Mean age: 62.3 ± 11.7 years
- Male/Female ratio: 142/103 (57.9% / 42.1%)
- Hypertension prevalence: 68.2% (n=167)
- Diabetes mellitus: 23.7% (n=58)
- Prior stroke/TIA: 8.6% (n=21)

CHA₂DS₂-VASc Score Distribution:
- Score 0-1: 31 patients (12.7%)
- Score 2-3: 89 patients (36.3%)
- Score ≥4: 125 patients (51.0%)

Anticoagulation at admission:
- NOAC: 156 (63.7%)
- Warfarin: 52 (21.2%)
- None: 37 (15.1%)

Primary outcome (ischemic stroke at 12 months): 14 events (5.7%)
- In NOAC group: 5/156 (3.2%)
- In warfarin group: 4/52 (7.7%)
- In no-anticoagulation group: 5/37 (13.5%)

p-value (NOAC vs no anticoagulation): 0.012
p-value (warfarin vs no anticoagulation): 0.048

Conclusion: NOAC therapy was associated with significantly lower ischemic stroke
rates compared to no anticoagulation (p=0.012). Warfarin showed a trend toward
benefit but did not reach the same level of significance when compared to NOACs.

Ethics approval: Ankara University Ethics Committee, Decision No: 2022/01/15-234
