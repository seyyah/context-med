# Chest Pain Triage

## Overview

Chest pain accounts for approximately 5-8% of all emergency department visits. Rapid triage is critical as acute coronary syndrome (ACS) requires intervention within 90 minutes of first medical contact.

**Source:** T.C. Ministry of Health Emergency Medicine Guidelines, 2024.

## Initial Assessment

1. **ABCs and vital signs** within 5 minutes of arrival.
2. **12-lead ECG** within 10 minutes.
3. **Focused history:** OPQRST mnemonic (Onset, Provocation, Quality, Radiation, Severity, Time).

## Red Flags

- Radiation to left arm, jaw, or back
- Diaphoresis (cold sweating)
- Dyspnea on exertion
- Duration >20 minutes, unresponsive to nitroglycerin
- History of MI or revascularization
- Age >55 with multiple cardiac risk factors

## Risk Stratification

**HEART Score** for chest pain evaluation:

| Component | 0 Points | 1 Point | 2 Points |
|-----------|----------|---------|----------|
| History | Slightly suspicious | Moderately suspicious | Highly suspicious |
| ECG | Normal | Non-specific changes | Significant ST deviation |
| Age | <45 | 45-64 | ≥65 |
| Risk factors | None | 1-2 | ≥3 or known CAD |
| Troponin | Normal | 1-3× normal | >3× normal |

- Score 0-3: Low risk (observe, possible discharge)
- Score 4-6: Moderate risk (admit, serial troponins)
- Score 7-10: High risk (urgent intervention)

**Source:** HEART Score validation, Backus et al., 2013.
