# Breast Cancer

## Overview

Breast cancer is the most commonly diagnosed cancer in women worldwide, with approximately 2.3 million new cases annually (2022 data). It is the leading cause of cancer-related mortality in women.

**Source:** GLOBOCAN 2022, International Agency for Research on Cancer.

## Risk Factors

- **Age:** Risk increases after age 50.
- **Genetic:** BRCA1/BRCA2 mutations account for 5-10% of cases.
- **Hormonal:** Early menarche, late menopause, nulliparity.
- **Lifestyle:** Obesity (postmenopausal), alcohol consumption, physical inactivity.

## HER2 Status

HER2 (Human Epidermal Growth Factor Receptor 2) overexpression is found in approximately 15-20% of breast cancers. Determined by:
1. Immunohistochemistry (IHC): 3+ is positive; 2+ requires FISH confirmation.
2. Fluorescence in situ hybridization (FISH): Gene amplification ratio ≥2.0.

**Source:** ASCO/CAP HER2 Testing Guideline, 2023.

## Treatment Options (Triple-Negative)

Triple-negative breast cancer (TNBC) lacks ER, PR, and HER2 expression (~15% of cases).

- **Neoadjuvant chemotherapy:** Anthracycline + taxane backbone.
- **Immunotherapy:** Pembrolizumab + chemotherapy (KEYNOTE-522 trial, pCR improvement from 51.2% to 64.8%).
- **PARP inhibitors:** Olaparib for germline BRCA+ patients (OlympiAD trial).

**Source:** KEYNOTE-522, Schmid et al., NEJM 2022.
